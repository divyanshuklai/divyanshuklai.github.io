Smart Waste Segregator

What Went Right

While the project wasn't a complete success, it was a great learning experience. Here are a few things that went as planned:

  • The LIDAR-based detection system was a good choice, it was more efficient than continuous video processing and helped keep the Raspberry Pi from overheating.
  • The metal MG995 servos were robust enough for the segregation mechanism, although they required a dedicated power supply.
  • A physical on/off switch was a small but useful addition.
  • I was able to reduce the latency to under 3 seconds by using a smaller model (EfficientNet), which was a good optimization.

What Went Wrong

This project was a valuable lesson in the difference between theory and practice. Here are some of the key challenges and failures:

  • The model's real-world performance was disappointing. It was heavily biased towards classifying everything as biodegradable due to a skewed dataset.
  • I spent a significant amount of time troubleshooting hardware issues, like getting the PiCamera to work through the terminal. This took time away from collecting a diverse and representative dataset.
  • The lack of a proper test set of images from the actual PiCamera meant that I didn't have a good way to evaluate the model's performance in a real-world scenario.
  • The online datasets I used for training didn't accurately represent the kind of waste the bin would encounter in reality.
